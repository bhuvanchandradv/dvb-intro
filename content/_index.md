I'm Bhuvan Chandra, a Senior Systems Engineer specializing in Mechatronics and Embedded Systems, currently based in Tokyo, Japan, where I work at [Rapyuta Robotics](https://www.rapyuta-robotics.com/) on autonomous warehouse robotics.

My work spans the full embedded stack — from real-time firmware and motor control to system architecture and reliability engineering. When I'm not deep in hardware debugging, I'm designing systems, mentoring engineers, and shaping how we build embedded software as a team.

I'm on a trajectory toward Systems Architecture, thinking about how complex robotic systems fit together at scale — and I have a growing interest in developer tooling and AI-assisted workflows that make embedded teams more effective.

Before Tokyo, I built my foundation at [Toradex](https://www.toradex.com/) working across industrial embedded platforms and communication protocols. That background in real hardware constraints — timing, memory, determinism — still shapes how I approach every problem. Along the way I made upstream contributions to the [Linux kernel](https://git.kernel.org/pub/scm/linux/kernel/git/torvalds/linux.git/log/?qt=author&q=bhuvanchandra.dv) and [U-Boot](http://git.denx.de/?p=u-boot.git&a=search&h=refs%2Fheads%2Fmaster&st=author&s=bhuvanchandra.dv).

I write about embedded systems, robotics, real-time OS internals, and the craft of building reliable systems in production environments.
